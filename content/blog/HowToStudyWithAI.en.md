## 00_Intro

Hello!

AI's growth has been fast almost beyond words.<br>
In this situation, it even makes me feel that we're now living not in an `era of using AI`, but in the `era of AI` itself.

Amid this atmosphere, we have to **adapt** quickly and constantly **think hard** about how to keep pace.<br>

So today, I'll cover what I've been thinking, and what the **most important thing** is when studying through AI.
<br><br>

---

## 01_A World Where You Can Become a Hacker If You Want To

I was born in 2004, which puts me among the younger ones in the information-security field.<br>
So I didn't personally live through the era when people I admire — like `Hellsonic` and `PPP` — were studying, but I'm certainly aware that it was quite different from today.

That "old era" we all talk about.<br>
Back then, people really looked up everything from A to Z themselves, read books, and acquired skills through repeated failure.<br>


But what about now? <br>

If there's something you don't know, we've arrived at a world where a **single line of prompt** solves it. With that one line, we can become hackers, become lawyers, and even become doctors.<br>
This is a tremendous **opportunity** for all of us, and at the same time it has created a **gap** you can never catch up to if you fail to adapt.

A representative example of this can be seen in `CTF`, too.
<br><br>

---

## 02_The Mass Emergence of All-Rounders

Just three years ago, when going to a CTF, it was taken for granted to build a team with one person each for `pwn`, `web`, `rev`, and `forensics`.<br>
That tendency still exists today, but it's true that it has grown very, very weak.

In fact, you often see people enter a competition as a one-person team and win with **countless Agents**, or people who aren't even good at hacking landing in the prize ranks. It's not without reason that the phrase `pay-to-win` comes to mind🥲

Put nicely, they're `people who are good at using AI`.<br>
Put unkindly, they're `click monkeys`. <br>

Personally, I tend to see them very **positively**. <br>
Let me keep talking about how someone like me thinks about the current era.
<br><br>

---

## 03_Use It Well, Leverage It Well. But Be Sure to Understand.

I got into security through the fields of digital forensics and web. And after that, at BoB I did forensics as well.<br>
If this were an era without AI, I think I would have just gotten a job like that and made a living off it for the rest of my life. But the me of today, with AI, does reversing too, and sometimes analyzes vulnerabilities and writes exploits.<br><br>

When you're with AI, this is the biggest **advantage**.<br>
It's wonderful for broadening the **Volume** of your knowledge on any subject. It's almost odd to say you don't know some field.

But the more you use AI, the harder it becomes to grow **Depth**.<br>
Because you can find knowledge so effortlessly, you don't try to engrave it in your head and remember it. Even I, writing this, am guilty of that,,
<br>

That's why I believe that, for us who work alongside AI, the process of "**understanding**" matters most.<br>

When you read a book yourself, understanding and memory happened at the same time in the process of finding knowledge, but now that's hard.<br>
So the key seems to be not `'don't know → look it up'` as the end, but `'don't know → looked it up → understand + record'`.

The reason recording matters is that anyone can say slick, polished things with AI.<br>
The traces proving that you were genuinely curious about this, looked it up, and worked to internalize it will surely become a great strength for your future!!
<br><br>

## 04_Outro

To sum it all up:
```text
If you don't know something, look it up with AI. But always be sure to "understand" it, and next time, make it something you can explain on your own!!
```
